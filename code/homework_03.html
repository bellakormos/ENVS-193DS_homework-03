Problem 1. Slough soil salinity

a. An appropriate test

To determine the strength of the relationship between salinity and California pickleweed biomass, the two appropriate tests are the Pearson correlation and Spearman correlation tests. The pearson correlation (correlation coefficient, r) measures the strength between soil salinity and biomass, and assumes that variables are continuous, normally distributed, and have independent observations. The Spearman rank correlation is a non-parametric test that measures the stength of the monotonic relationship between ranked values of soil salinity and biomass, and it does not assume normality.

Written response: I checked whether soil salinity and pickleweed biomass were normally distributed, since Pearson’s r assumes normality. I used the Shapiro-Wilk test for both variables, and the test showed p = 0.46 for soil salinity and p = 0.11 for pickleweed biomass. Since both p-values are greater than 0.05, the data does not significantly deviate from normality, and the Pearson correlation is appropriate.

Running the Test

# run Pearson correlation between soil salinity and pickleweed biomass
cor.test(salinity$salinity_mS_cm, # predictor: soil salinity
         salinity$pickleweed, # response: pickleweed biomass
